The Regulatory Reality · Country by Country

European drone permits: an obstacle course.

EU/EASA frameworks are standardizing, but individual countries enforce their own local laws, municipal permit requirements, and severe financial penalties — making commercial drone shoots impractical in most premium real estate markets.

🇪🇸 Spain
AESA · Royal Decree 517/2024
Cost €89.59 non-refundable application fee + €480/day public space occupation in Barcelona
Wait 15–30 working days for municipal permit from local town hall
Required Commercial operator registration with AESA + Licencia de Ocupación for any public space flight

A coastal villa listing cannot wait a month for a permit while active buyers move on.

🇩🇪 Germany
LuftVO § 21h · Federal Drone Law
Prohibited Camera drones are strictly prohibited over residential properties without written permission from every neighboring landowner
GDPR fine Up to €50,000 for capturing neighbors' gardens or windows — plus potential criminal prosecution under BDSG
Required Mandatory commercial third-party liability insurance + individual written consent from all adjacent landowners

One neighbor complaint exposes an agency to five-figure fines and civil litigation — even with a licensed pilot.

🇫🇷 France
DGAC · Specific Category S3 Scenario
Fine €450 – €750 per infraction for unauthorized commercial flights or failing to register a camera drone
Required S3 operator authorization: pilot licenses, operational manuals, and individual préfecture declarations for each urban zone
Reality Flying over populated zones without S3 clearance is illegal by default — and most premium property zones qualify

Paris, Lyon, the Côte d'Azur — the paperwork cost regularly exceeds the commercial value of the drone footage itself.

🇨🇭 Switzerland
BAZL · A1/A2/A3 Subcategory Framework
Insurance Mandatory liability coverage of at minimum CHF 1,000,000 per commercial flight
Fine Unauthorized flights near premium zones scale to CHF 20,000
Airspace Dense population means almost every premium real estate zone is near restricted airspace — requiring active clearances from airports and helipads

A1/A3 and A2 online certification exams are mandatory before any commercial flight — and every premium listing zone adds a new clearance hurdle.
